The Keto-Asian Shop

Building a keto-friendly Asian pantry is about finding the right anchors. This is the gear and the ingredients I actually use in my kitchen to keep things fast, low-carb, and full of soul.

🥢 The Cooking Gear & Serving

You don't need a thousand gadgets, but these few tools make the rhythm of stir-frying, steaming, and serving much easier.

  • Carbon Steel Wok

    The heart of the kitchen. High heat, fast cooking, and develops a natural non-stick patina over time. Perfect for everything from Pad Krapao to Suan La Báicài.

  • Donabe (Japanese Earthenware Pot)

    The ultimate vessel for Shabu-Shabu, Sukiyaki, and Tori Nabe. It retains heat beautifully, making it perfect for slow-simmering hot pots that stay warm at the table.

  • Airtight Glass Jars (1 Liter)

    Essential for your Homemade Kimchi. These jars allow you to pack the cabbage tightly and see the fermentation process in action. I recommend clip-tops or sturdy gaskets.

  • Glass Fermentation Weights

    The secret to safe kimchi. These keep your cabbage fully submerged under the brine, preventing oxygen from reaching the vegetables and ensuring a perfect ferment.

  • Tamagoyaki Omelet Pan (Square)

    To get those perfect rectangular layers in your Tamagoyaki, a square non-stick pan makes rolling much easier than a round skillet.

  • Bamboo Sushi Rolling Mats

    Necessary for getting a tight, clean roll on your Egg Kimbap. Inexpensive and effective.

  • Bamboo Steamer Basket

    Essential for Cabbage-Wrapped Chicken Dumplings or steamed fish. Fits right over a wok or standard pot.

  • Extra Long Cooking Chopsticks

    Great for scrambling eggs, tossing noodles, or moving dumplings — reach and precision without burning your hands.

  • Ceramic Ramen Bowls (Large)

    Deep bowls change the experience — roomy enough for a full serving of ramen with toppings.

  • Miso Soup Bowls (Lacquerware)

    Traditional small bowls for your Tamagoyaki & Miso Set. Lidded bowls keep dashi hot while you finish your omelet.

  • Japanese Serving Plates & Small Dishes

    For serving Banchan, Kimchi, or Smashed Cucumber. Small ceramic plates make a simple keto lunch feel like a set meal.

🧂 The Konjac (Shirataki) Collection

Konjac is your best friend on keto — pure fiber that carries sauces without the carbs.

  • Flat Style Konjac Noodles (Fettuccine/Tagliatelle) — great for Phở Gà or soups where a wider bite is preferred.
  • Round Konjac Noodles (Spaghetti/Angel Hair) — bouncy texture, best when dry-fried first.
  • Konjac Rice — a useful rice substitute for soaking up rich stews like Mapo Tofu.

🍯 Keto Sweeteners & Pantry Staples

Swap the sugar for these keto-safe alternatives that don't spike insulin.

Why these sweeteners?

In Wok This Way I often used Sukrin Gold because it's easy to find in Europe. When shopping on Amazon, however, Allulose and Monkfruit blends are the best options for Asian cooking.

Allulose is a game-changer for keto-glazed dishes. While regular erythritol (like Sukrin) can crystallize in cold sauces, Allulose dissolves completely and can even caramelize — the secret to a glossy finish on dishes like Miso-Glazed Salmon.

Monkfruit & Erythritol blends (e.g., Lakanto) give a clean, neutral sweetness. They work excellently to balance salt and heat in a dashi or a quick stir-fry sauce without leaving a dominant aftertaste.

  • White Miso Paste (Shiro Miso) — mellow and slightly sweet; great for miso-glazed salmon and creamy ramen.
  • Allulose Sweetener — dissolves and caramelizes, ideal for sticky glazes without cooling aftertaste.
  • Monkfruit & Erythritol Blend — clean white sugar replacement for light dressings and dashi balancing.

🌊 Umami & Flavor Builders

  • Roasted Nori Sheets — use for furikake or ramen garnish.
  • Toasted Sesame Seeds — for goma dressing and spinach namul.
  • Red Boat Fish Sauce — anchovies and salt; no added sugar.
  • Dried Shiitake Mushrooms — rehydrate for stir-fries or use soaking liquid for broths.
  • Sichuan Peppercorns — toast and grind for Mapo Tofu or Yu Xiang Qiezi.
  • Toban Djan (Chili Bean Paste) — salty, spicy, and funky; essential for Mapo Tofu.
  • Lao Gan Ma Chili Crisp — classic chili crisp; a little goes a long way.
